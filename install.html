Welcome to X-Mega-Saw (XMS) — Before You Begin – Safety First! Unplug everything: Make sure your PC is off and disconnected from power. Avoid static electricity: Work on a wooden table, not carpet. Touch metal (like your PC case) to discharge static, or wear an anti-static wristband. Have tools ready: You’ll need a Phillips screwdriver, and your motherboard manual (very helpful!).

Installing Your XMS Gaming PC

This guide will walk you through installing and assembling your X-Mega-Saw (XMS) gaming PC. Based on real research and teen-led testing, these steps are designed to be simple, clear, and safe—even for first-time builders.

  • Tools Needed: Phillips screwdriver, anti-static band (optional), motherboard manual.
  • Time: 1 to 2 hours for a full install.
  • Tip: Don’t rush—go step-by-step and double-check connections.

Step 1: Safety & Setup

Start with a clean, static-free workspace.

  • Work on a wooden surface or desk—not carpet.
  • Unplug your PC power supply if you're upgrading or reassembling.
  • Touch metal or wear an anti-static wristband to discharge any static.
  • Lay out your components before starting.

Step 2: Prep the Motherboard

Kobe, our XMS motherboard expert, recommends installing these parts before mounting:

  • CPU: Open the socket, align the triangle on the CPU, and gently place it in.
  • RAM: Push firmly into the memory slots until it clicks.
  • M.2 SSD (optional): Insert at an angle, push flat, and screw in.

Step 3: Mount the Motherboard

Now that your CPU and RAM are in, it's time to mount the board in the case:

  • Install the I/O shield (metal backplate) into the back of your case.
  • Align the motherboard with screw holes and I/O ports.
  • Screw the board into place using 6–9 screws depending on form factor (ATX/mATX).

Step 4: Connect Cables

Time to connect everything to power it up:

  • 24-pin motherboard power cable from PSU.
  • 8-pin CPU power cable (top of board).
  • Front panel connectors: power button, USB, audio (check your case and motherboard manual).
  • Fan headers for CPU and case cooling.

Step 5: Final Check & First Boot

You’re nearly there! Let’s power up and test:

  • Double-check all cable connections.
  • Connect monitor, keyboard, and mouse.
  • Turn on the power switch at the back of the PSU.
  • Press the power button on your case. If you see the BIOS screen—success!

Troubleshooting Tips

If your PC doesn't turn on or shows no display, try this:

  • No power? Recheck PSU switch, 24-pin cable, and CPU cable.
  • No display? Make sure the monitor is plugged into the GPU, not the motherboard (if using a dedicated GPU).
  • Red light on motherboard? Check RAM or CPU placement.

If you're stuck, search your motherboard model + issue on YouTube or tech forums—or ask the XMS team!
