Welcome to X-Mega-Saw (XMS) — Simple Motherboard Buyer’s Guide for Gamers Whether you're building a gaming PC from scratch or upgrading, the motherboard is a key part. Think of it as the “base” that connects your CPU, RAM, graphics card (GPU), storage, and more. Picking the right one depends on your CPU brand (Intel or AMD), your budget, and what features you need.

Getting Started with XMS

Welcome to the official guide for building your own gaming PC with X-Mega-Saw (XMS)! This guide will help you understand each part, how to choose it, how to buy safely, and how to put it all together. Built by teens, for teens—this guide is beginner-friendly and based on real NZ prices and parts.

  • Who it's for: Beginners and gamers building their first PC.
  • What you’ll learn: Part selection, safety tips, installation steps, and trusted sellers.

Choosing Your PC Parts

The XMS team selected each part based on budget, performance, and compatibility. Here's a breakdown of what to consider when choosing your own:

  • Motherboard: The foundation – choose one that matches your CPU and RAM (DDR4 or DDR5).
  • CPU & GPU: These control speed and graphics. Match with your motherboard chipset.
  • RAM: 16GB DDR4 is enough for most games. DDR5 is faster for future-proofing.
  • Storage: SSD for fast loading, HDD for big game libraries.
  • Cooling & Case: Keeps parts cool and organized.

Safe Buying Guide (NZ & Online)

Whether you’re buying from a store or online, new or used, follow these safety tips:

  • Use trusted NZ sellers: PB Tech, Mighty Ape, Computer Lounge.
  • Buying used? Use Trade Me or Facebook Marketplace—but check reviews, photos, and ask questions.
  • Match your parts: Check socket types, RAM types, and form factor (ATX/mATX).
  • Use safe payments: PayPal or debit/credit card. Avoid cash or bank transfers unless you trust the seller.
  • Check for warranty: New parts usually come with at least 12 months protection.
